LINCOLN UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT
BOARD OF EDUCATION — REGULAR MEETING MINUTES
February 11, 2026

POLICY UPDATES APPROVED:

Policy 6140 — CURRICULUM FRAMEWORK (Amended)
- Core subjects: English, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, Computer Science (NEW)
- Electives: Art, Music, Physical Education, Foreign Language, AI Literacy (NEW)
- Technology integration: 1:1 Chromebook program for grades 3-12 (replacing computer labs)
- Class size: Maximum 25 students per classroom (reduced from 30)
- Textbook adoption cycle: Every 4 years (accelerated from 6)
- Standardized testing: State assessments in grades 3, 5, 8 + NEW district benchmark assessments quarterly
- NEW: Project-based learning requirement — minimum 2 cross-disciplinary projects per semester
- NEW: Digital citizenship curriculum mandatory for all grades

Policy 5131 — STUDENT CONDUCT & SAFETY (Amended)
- Zero-tolerance policy for weapons and controlled substances
- Bullying prevention: Comprehensive anti-bullying program (expanded from awareness week)
- NEW: Cyberbullying policy — includes social media conduct outside school hours
- Cell phone policy: Devices collected at start of day, returned at dismissal (strengthened)
- Visitor policy: Sign-in at front office + photo ID required + background check for volunteers
- Emergency drills: Fire drill monthly, lockdown drill monthly (increased from quarterly)
- NEW: Mental health screening — annual wellness check for all students grades 6-12
- NEW: Threat assessment team established at each school site

Policy 4112 — STAFF DEVELOPMENT (Amended)
- Professional development: 40 hours annually required (doubled from 20)
- NEW: AI and technology training — 10 hours of the 40 must be tech-focused
- Teacher evaluation: Twice annually with student feedback component (expanded)
- Substitute teacher requirements: State credential + district orientation required
- Staff-to-student ratio: 1:22 for general education (improved from 1:25)
- NEW: Teacher mentorship program — all first-year teachers assigned mentor

Policy 3100 — BUDGET ALLOCATION (Amended)
- Per-pupil spending: $11,500 per student (increased from $9,200)
- Technology budget: $750,000 annually (5x increase for 1:1 program)
- Facilities maintenance: $500,000 reserve fund
- Transportation: 15 bus routes serving district (3 new routes added)
- Free/reduced lunch program: Automatic enrollment via direct certification (simplified)
- NEW: Mental health services budget: $200,000 for counselors and programs
- NEW: Teacher recruitment bonus: $5,000 signing bonus for STEM teachers

Policy 6163 — SPECIAL EDUCATION (Amended)
- IEP review cycle: Semi-annual (increased from annual)
- Inclusion model: Full inclusion with push-in services as default (changed from pull-out)
- Aide staffing: 1 aide per 6 students with IEPs (improved from 1:8)
- Assistive technology: Proactive assessment for all IEP students (changed from upon-request)
- NEW: Transition planning begins at age 14 (previously age 16)
- NEW: Universal Design for Learning (UDL) framework adopted district-wide
