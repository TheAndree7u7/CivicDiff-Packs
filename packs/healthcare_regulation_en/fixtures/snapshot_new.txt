STATE HEALTHCARE COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK — Version 4.0
Effective: March 1, 2026

Section 4.1 — TELEHEALTH SERVICES (Expanded)
- Telehealth permitted for ALL visit types including initial consultations (expanded)
- Audio-only visits permitted for behavioral health and rural patients (NEW)
- Provider must be licensed in patient's state OR hold interstate compact license (expanded)
- Prescriptions via telehealth: All patients including new patients with proper verification (expanded)
- Documentation: Enhanced telehealth-specific notes with technology verification
- NEW: Remote patient monitoring (RPM) covered for chronic conditions
- NEW: AI-assisted triage permitted with physician oversight

Section 4.2 — DATA SECURITY (HIPAA+ Enhanced)
- Encryption: AES-256 minimum for data at rest (upgraded from AES-128)
- Access controls: Role-based + multi-factor authentication required (enhanced)
- Audit logs: Retained for 6 years (increased from 3)
- Breach notification: Within 30 days of discovery (reduced from 60)
- Risk assessment: Semi-annual requirement (increased from annual)
- Business Associate Agreements: Required + annual security attestation (enhanced)
- NEW: AI model governance — all clinical AI must have bias audit and explainability documentation
- NEW: Patient data portability — support FHIR R4 API within 12 months

Section 4.3 — PATIENT SAFETY REPORTING (Strengthened)
- Adverse events: Report within 24 hours (reduced from 72)
- Near-miss reporting: MANDATORY real-time reporting (changed from voluntary)
- Root cause analysis: Required for sentinel events + serious safety events (expanded)
- Patient safety committee: Monthly meetings (increased from quarterly)
- NEW: Patient safety dashboard — real-time incident tracking required
- NEW: Transparency reporting — annual public safety report required

Section 4.4 — STAFFING REQUIREMENTS (Enhanced)
- Nurse-to-patient ratio: 1:4 for medical-surgical units (improved from 1:6)
- Physician supervision: 24/7 availability required (expanded from operating hours)
- Continuing education: 40 hours annually per clinician (increased from 30)
- Credentialing: Every 2 years (shortened from 3)
- NEW: Burnout prevention — mandatory wellness program for all clinical staff
- NEW: Diversity requirements — cultural competency training 8 hours annually

Section 4.5 — BILLING AND COMPLIANCE (Tightened)
- Claim submission deadline: 60 days from service (reduced from 90)
- Prior authorization: AI-assisted pre-auth permitted; decision within 48 hours (NEW timeline)
- Audit frequency: Random 10% sample annually (doubled from 5%)
- Penalty for fraudulent claims: Up to $100,000 per incident (doubled)
- NEW: Price transparency — machine-readable pricing files required
- NEW: Surprise billing protections — no balance billing for emergency services
