ORDINANCE 2026-001: ZONING AMENDMENT — DOWNTOWN OVERLAY DISTRICT (SECOND READING)
Second reading and adoption of Ordinance 2026-001, amending Chapter 14 (Zoning) of the Municipal Code to establish a Downtown Mixed-Use Overlay District.

AMENDMENTS ADOPTED SINCE FIRST READING:
1. Affordable housing requirement INCREASED from 10% to 15% for developments exceeding 20 units
2. Parking requirement RESTORED to 1.25 spaces per unit (compromise from original 1.5 and proposed 1.0)
3. NEW: Mandatory Transportation Demand Management (TDM) plan required for developments over 50 units
4. NEW: Small business protection clause — existing commercial tenants given 18-month relocation assistance if displaced by redevelopment
5. Ground-floor commercial frontage requirement REDUCED from 60% to 50% for parcels under 5,000 sq ft
6. NEW: Green building standards — all new construction must meet LEED Silver equivalent or higher
7. Height limit INCREASED from 6 stories to 8 stories for parcels fronting Main Street only

Discussion:
- CM Garcia praised the small business protection amendment but expressed concern about enforcement.
- CM Thompson accepted the parking compromise but requested a 2-year parking impact study.
- CM Park moved to add a sunset review clause — overlay district provisions to be reviewed in 5 years.
- CM Williams raised concern about the height increase impact on adjacent residential neighborhoods.

Motion by CM Nakamura, seconded by CM Davis, to adopt Ordinance 2026-001 as amended.
Vote: 5-2 (CM Thompson, CM Williams opposed). Motion carried.

EFFECTIVE DATE: March 7, 2026 (30 days after adoption)

ORDINANCE 2026-004: HEALTH IMPACT ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENT (FIRST READING)
First reading of Ordinance 2026-004, amending Chapter 8 (Health and Safety) to require Health Impact Assessments (HIAs) for:
- Any industrial development exceeding 25,000 square feet
- Any warehouse or distribution facility within 0.5 miles of residential zones
- Any project requiring an Environmental Impact Report under state law

Key provisions:
- HIA must be conducted by a qualified independent assessor approved by the City
- Cost borne by the applicant
- City Health Officer to review and provide recommendations within 60 days
- Council may impose health-protective conditions based on HIA findings
- Appeals process through the Planning Commission

City Health Officer Dr. Raymond Cruz presented the staff report and health data supporting the requirement.

No vote taken (first reading). Second reading scheduled for March 4, 2026.

RESOLUTION 2026-R-007: EMERGENCY WATER INFRASTRUCTURE REPAIR
Resolution declaring an emergency condition and authorizing expedited procurement for repair of the 36-inch water main beneath Riverside Boulevard.

Background:
- Main installed in 1978, last inspected 2019
- Recent inspection revealed critical corrosion at three joint locations
- Risk of catastrophic failure serving approximately 8,500 residential connections
- Estimated repair cost: $1,200,000
- Funding source: Water Enterprise Fund reserves

Motion by CM Davis, seconded by CM Williams, to adopt Resolution 2026-R-007.
Vote: 7-0. Motion carried.

TIMELINE: Emergency procurement to begin immediately. Repair work estimated 8-12 weeks. Temporary water service disruptions expected for affected areas with 72-hour advance notice.
