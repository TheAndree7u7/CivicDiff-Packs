ORDINANCE 2026-001: ZONING AMENDMENT — DOWNTOWN OVERLAY DISTRICT
First reading of Ordinance 2026-001, amending Chapter 14 (Zoning) of the Municipal Code to establish a Downtown Mixed-Use Overlay District.

Key provisions:
- Applies to parcels within the area bounded by Main Street, 5th Avenue, River Road, and 12th Avenue
- Permits mixed-use development (residential above commercial) up to 6 stories
- Requires 10% affordable housing units in developments exceeding 20 units
- Minimum ground-floor commercial space: 60% of frontage
- Parking requirement: 1 space per unit (reduced from 1.5)
- Design review required for all new construction

Planning Director James Morrison presented the staff report. The Planning Commission voted 5-2 to recommend approval on December 10, 2025.

Discussion:
- CM Thompson raised concerns about parking adequacy with the reduced requirement.
- CM Nakamura asked about the affordable housing enforcement mechanism.
- Planning Director Morrison confirmed that a housing trust fund deposit would be required as an alternative to on-site affordable units.

No vote taken (first reading). Second reading scheduled for February 5, 2026.

RESOLUTION 2026-R-003: ANNUAL FEE SCHEDULE UPDATE
Resolution updating municipal fees and charges for fiscal year 2026-2027.

Notable changes:
- Building permit fees: increased 3% (from $45 to $46.35 per $1,000 valuation)
- Business license renewal: increased from $150 to $175
- Park pavilion rental: increased from $200 to $225/day
- Water connection fee: unchanged at $2,500
- Sewer connection fee: unchanged at $1,800
- Fire inspection fee: increased from $100 to $125
- Street excavation permit: increased from $300 to $350

Finance Director Lisa Park presented the annual fee comparison study.

Motion by CM Williams, seconded by CM Davis, to adopt Resolution 2026-R-003.
Vote: 5-1 (CM Garcia opposed). Motion carried.
